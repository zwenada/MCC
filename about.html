About MCC and What are We

Myanmar Community Club (MCC) at James Cook University Singapore was founded by Ko Kyaw Phyo Aung, a former student known for his leadership and active role in the community. Since its founding, MCC has been a welcoming space for both new and current students, creating a sense of belonging far from home. Our club organizes various events, from traditional celebrations to chill nights, bringing Myanmar culture to life and offering students a chance to relax and connect. Led by dedicated exco members, who are committed students themselves, MCC is more than just a club—it’s a family. We understand the struggles of homesickness and academic stress, and we’re always here to support Myanmar students through these challenges. Together, we create a strong, supportive community where everyone feels at home.

We are here to help you

At Myanmar Community Club (MCC), we understand the challenges that come with starting a new journey at university. Whether you're a new student trying to settle in or a current student seeking support, we're here to help. From navigating campus life to connecting with resources, our dedicated team is always ready to provide guidance. No question is too small—reach out to us anytime, and we'll be happy to assist you in making your experience as smooth and enjoyable as possible. Together, we can build a strong, supportive community.
